Brazilian Brigadeiro

Brigadeiro is a traditional Brazilian sweet that is popularly enjoyed during parties and celebrations. It is a small, round, chocolate truffle-like confection that is made with condensed milk, cocoa powder, and butter.

Traditionally, brigadeiros are coated in chocolate sprinkles, but other toppings like shredded coconut, chopped nuts, or powdered sugar can also be used. Brigadeiros are rich, fudgy, and have a texture that is similar to that of a chocolate truffle.

Brigadeiros are a beloved treat in Brazil and are often served at birthday parties, weddings, and other special events. They are a simple yet delicious dessert that is sure to satisfy anyone's sweet tooth.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • Chocolate sprinkles or other toppings of your choice

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the sweetened condensed milk, cocoa powder, and butter.
  2. Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon, until it starts to thicken and pull away from the sides of the pan. This should take around 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove the pan from heat and let the mixture cool to room temperature.
  4. Once cooled, use a spoon or cookie scoop to form the mixture into small balls, about the size of a cherry.
  5. Roll the balls in chocolate sprinkles or your desired toppings until completely coated.
  6. Place the brigadeiros on a plate or in a container lined with wax paper, making sure they are not touching each other.
  7. Chill the brigadeiros in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, or until firm.
  8. Serve and enjoy!
